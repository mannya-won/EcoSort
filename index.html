Different Types Of Waste Management

HAZARDOUS WASTE

Toxic or dangerous materials like chemicals and batteries that require special disposal to prevent harm

E-WASTE

Discarded electronics containing harmful substances, requiring recycling to reduce pollution and recover materials

MEDICAL WASTE

Infectious or biohazardous waste from healthcare facilities, needing careful segregation and disposal

BIODEGRADABLE WASTE

Organic materials like food scraps and garden waste that decompose naturally, enriching the soil

What is waste management and why is it important??

Waste segregation is the systematic division of waste into distinct categories to ensure efficient disposal, recycling, and treatment. This process lies at the heart of sustainable waste management practices and directly impacts environmental health, economic sustainability, and public welfare. Waste segregation involves separating waste into biodegradable, non-biodegradable, recyclable, and hazardous categories. Commonly used color-coded bins, such as green for organic waste, blue for recyclables, and red or yellow for hazardous materials, facilitate this process. Proper segregation starts at the source—homes, industries, schools, and offices—making it a collective responsibility
